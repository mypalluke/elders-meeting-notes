Agenda Item 2

Shepherding the Groups

How can Group Overseers & assistants coordinate dynamically to reach inactive or irregular friends with genuine Christian empathy? These are my 6 primary focus points for the body:

1

Set the Example of Zeal

Actions speak louder than plans. Overseers and assistants must lead the way by displaying joy and active participation in the field ministry, making it naturally appealing for others to join.

2

People, Not Checkboxes

Do not treat inactive or irregular ones as records to update. We must deeply love our brothers and sisters. Never let a visit or call feel like a routine administration check-off.

3

Deep Interest in Their Lives

Take the time to listen and care. Ask sincerely about their health, their family, and their day-to-day challenges before attempting to discuss attendance, meeting links, or service.

4

Coordinate with Assistant

Collaborate closely. The group overseer and assistant should map out their specific group list together to identify who needs practical help, separating administrative lists to focus on target needs.

5

Check Missing Reports Wisely

Some friends might simply have forgotten to turn in their monthly reports. Confirm this gently before concluding someone has drifted away or requires an intensive shepherding intervention.

6

Avoid Being a "Busybody"

Respect personal privacy and maintain clean boundaries. Avoid prying or asking overly invasive questions about personal affairs. Truly show them we care for them with respect.

Agenda Item 3

Assess Allegation

Pending Investigative Report

An allegation was received that Individual X is struggling with alcoholism. Two brothers were sent to investigate, and we are awaiting their official report. Our goal at this meeting is to analyze their findings to see if the criteria exist to form a judicial committee.

Scriptural Blueprint (1 Cor 6:9, 10; 5:11)

"But now I am writing you to stop keeping company with anyone called a brother who is... a drunkard... not even eating with such a man."

Definition of Drunkenness: The state of losing command of mental and physical faculties due to overindulging in alcoholic beverages. A drunkard is one who habitually or practice-wise overindulges.

Judicial Assessment Criteria

What is a "Practice of Drunkenness"?

A repetitive, ongoing pattern of overindulgence to the point of complete intoxication. It cannot be classified as a single, isolated accidental misstep. We must find out from the report if there are multiple verifiable instances establishing a habit.

What is "Notoriety"?

The degree to which the wrong behavior has become known outside of the private setting. Has it brought clear reproach on the congregation? Is it widely talked about in the community or congregation, or was it confined to a private home context?

Checklist For Listening to the Report

Check off criteria as the two brothers detail their investigation:

Decision Guideline: If the facts show a clear, unrepentant practice or public notoriety, we form a judicial committee. If it is a private, isolated failure with deep sorrow, we focus on shepherdly assistance.
